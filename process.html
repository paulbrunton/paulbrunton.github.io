The Sculptural Journey

Every sculpture by Paul Brunton embarks on a meticulous journey from raw concept to finished form. It's a dialogue between intuition and technique, material and vision. Here's a glimpse into the creative process:

01

Conception & Sketching

The journey begins with an idea, often sparked by a found object, a natural form, or a philosophical concept. Paul translates these initial inspirations into detailed sketches, exploring contours, mass, and potential movement. This is where the abstract vision starts to take a tangible shape on paper.

02

Material Selection & Preparation

Choosing the right material is crucial. Whether it's selecting a rough block of marble, sourcing specific alloys for bronze casting, or finding unique pieces of reclaimed timber, the material itself often dictates the final form. Preparation involves cutting, shaping, and sometimes treating the raw material to begin the sculpting process.

03

Shaping & Refinement

This is the heart of the sculptural process. Using a combination of traditional tools and modern techniques, Paul meticulously carves, welds, casts, or constructs the piece. It's a highly iterative phase, where the form is slowly revealed, refined, and often re-imagined. Attention is paid to every curve, angle, and texture to achieve the desired emotional and visual impact.

04

Finishing & Patination

The final stages involve polishing, sanding, and applying various finishes. This process is crucial in giving the piece its unique surface narrative and protecting it for longevity.
